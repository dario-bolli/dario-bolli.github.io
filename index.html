About me

Welcome to my personal Website! My name is Dario, I am a final-year MSc student in the Department of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering at ETH Zurich.

Our brain, despite being at the core of our conscious experiences, remains shrouded in mystery, with much yet to be understood about its operations. My current focus lies in delving into the enigmatic workings of the human brain and leveraging those scientific insights to drive transformative and positive changes in society.

The objective of this pursuit is two-fold.

Firstly, these insights present promising avenues for the development of groundbreaking diagnostic tools, therapies, and innovative rehabilitation techniques aimed at addressing chronic neurological conditions. Furthermore, they offer valuable glimpses into the fundamental principles of biological intelligence, which in turn propels advancements in the field of Artificial Intelligence. Concurrently, the utilization of AI allows for the exploration of established and novel scientific problems, as well as the resolution of societal and environmental challenges. It is a driving force in advancing research and problem-solving across various domains.

I am currently pursuing my master's thesis within the Bioengineering department at Imperial College London, under the expert guidance of Prof. Aldo Faisal . My research focuses on comprehending and developing computational models of motor-learning mechanisms in the hope of contributing to the general understanding of the brain, and to propose innovative non-invasive rehabilitation methods.     • Computational model
    of Motor-Learning

    To adress the gap between the real world and traditionally limited Motor Learning experiments, the real-world task of playing billiard was chosen as a paradigm to study the adaptation of the participants to visuomotor perturbation.
    The manipulation of the visual feedback was made possible through the use of an embodied virtual reality environment. The analysis of the participants behaviour to the perturbation revealed two distinct groups relying primarily on either reward-based feedback or error-based feedback to learn to adapt to the perturbation.
    My current work involves the comprehension and derivation of computational models of the reward-based and error-based mechanisms. We aim at using this model to contribute to the understanding of our brain, as well as iintroducing a non-invasive rehabilitation method through manipulation of the feedback.

    Training in Superposition

    Description

    To address the always greedier need of Deep Neural Networks for computational resources, we investigate training and inference in superposition. Exploiting the properties of Hyper-Dimensional Computing, our concept seeks to reduce computation by superposing multiple data examples in a single compressed representation, hence processing the examples simultaneously in the Network.

    We validate the proposed method on image classification on the CIFAR-100 Dataset, using ResNet-12 as a base Classification Network. We first investigate the impact of binding and unbinding operations on the input and output of the ResNet-12. Next, we add a bundling operation to combine the inputs before processing them through the Classification Network.

    Our Results show that the Hyper-Dimensional Computing method does not allow to process multiple images together. Therefore, we modify the initial architecture, and test several data-driven methods derived from this hypothesis.

    We also provide an analysis of the image classification accuracy relative to the number of computational operations saved by means of processing data in superposition. Our best architecture allows to divide by two the number of Multiply-Accumulate operations with a roughly 4.5% reduction in the image classification accuracy when compared with the original ResNet.

    Both the code and the report are confidential.

    Deep-Hedging

    Description

    Hedging is a risk management strategy employed to balance losses in investments by taking an opposite position in a related asset. Mathematical finance provides a tractable solution to hedging in a frictionless and "complete market" models with risk-neutral pricing and hedging. However, trading is subject to transaction costs, permanent market impact, and liquidity constraints in real markets, something not modelled by classical mathematical finance models.

    Deep hedging aims at modeling the trading decisions in the hedging strategies as neural networks. It provides a model-free and efficient solution, enabling us to consider alternative factors such as market frictions. Deep Hedging models' feature sets consist of hedging instruments' prices and can contain additional information such as trading signals, news analytics, or past hedging decisions. We investigated replacing a simple Neural Network by State of the Art Deep Learning Models.

    Deep Learning for
    Autonomous Driving

    Description

    The goal was to do Multi-task learning for semantics and depth estimation on images gathered by 2 cameras mounted on a car, and 3D Object Detection from a Point Cloud gathered by a Lidar, also mounted on the car. The project was split in the 3 following parts:

    1. Understanding Multimodal Driving Data

      That is, creating the visualisation tools for the 3D object detection and point cloud semantic segmentation given a LiDAR point cloud, corresponding RGB camera image, ground truth semantic labels and network bounding box predictions. And processing the Lidar point Cloud, to be able to identify each laser ID from the point cloud directly, and account for the distortion caused by the vehicle motion with the aid of GPS/IMU data.

      If you want to see more, you can have a look at the task, the report, and the code

    2. Multi-task learning for semantics and depth

      The two main tasks in this part are the semantic segmentation (i.e., the task of associating each pixel of an image with a class label, e.g., person, road, car, etc.) and the monocular depth estimation (i.e., the task of estimating the per-pixel depth of a scene from a single image). We worked on the Miniscape dataset. The idea is to train those tasks jointly using Convolutional Neural Networks(CNNs), as the learning of one of the task can be beneficial to solve the other task. We derive an optimal joint architecture based on a shared, separate Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling (ASPP) and separate Decoders to solve both tasks.

      If you want to see more, you can have a look at the task, the report, and the code

    3. 3D Object Detection from Lidar Point Clouds

      In the last part, we build a 2-stage 3D object detector to detect vehicles in autonomous driving scenes. Our object-detector draws 3D bounding boxes around each vehicle. The First stage, which is often referred to as the Region Proposal Network (RPN), is used to create coarse detection results from the irregular point cloud data. These initial detections are later refined in the second stage network to generate the final predictions. The idea behind such a 2-stage design is to process the entire point cloud in the first stage and to generate regions-of-interest (ROI), i.e. smaller pools of information at and around likely objects which later can be refined individually to form the final detection. In this project, the first stage was prebuilt and pretrained to save time. Therefore, we worked on the initial predictions (i.e. the results of the first stage RPN alongside the extracted features and point cloud data).

      If you want to see more, you can have a look at the task, the report, and the code
